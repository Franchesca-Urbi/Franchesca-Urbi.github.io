Gabriel José García Márquez

The man who wove magic into reality

Here's a time line of Gabriel García Márquez life:

  • 1927 - Born in Aracataca, Colombia. Here, he spends his formative years with his eccentric and storytelling grandparents, who deeply influence his imagination.
  • 1936 - The family relocates to Barranquilla, this move exposed him to a more urban environment, but the fantastical tales and folklore of his hometown, Aracataca, remain a cornerstone of his inspiration.
  • 1947 - Graduated high school and publishes his first short story, "La tercera resignación" (The Third Resignation), in the national newspaper El Espectador. Began studying law at the Universidad Nacional de Colombia in Bogotá, although his true passion lies in writing.
  • 1948 - Took a break from law school to work as a reporter for El Universal in Cartagena. Honed his journalistic skills and gains a firsthand perspective on Colombian society.
  • 1950 - Retured to Barranquilla and becomes a popular columnist for El Heraldo under the pen name "Septimus." Capturing the attention of local readers with his witty and insightful writing.
  • 1955 - Published his first novel, "La hojarasca" (Leaf Storm), a dark tale hinting at the magical realism style that would become his signature.
  • 1958 - Married Mercedes Barcha Pardo, his lifelong companion and muse.
  • 1967 - Published his magnum opus, "Cien años de soledad" (One Hundred Years of Solitude). This epic novel, blending reality with fantasy, takes the world by storm and establishes him as a major literary figure.
  • 1970 - Continued to write prolifically, publishing novels like "El otoño del patriarca" (The Autumn of the Patriarch) and "Crónica de una muerte anunciada" (Chronicle of a Death Foretold). He also becomes a vocal advocate for social justice in Latin America.
  • 1982 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his novels and short stories, in which the fantastic and the realistic are combined in a world richly endowed with imagination, reflecting a whole continent's social and cultural conflicts.
  • 1985 - Released "El amor en los tiempos del cólera" (Love in the Time of Cholera), a timeless love story that resonates with readers around the globe.
  • 2014 - Died in Mexico City at the age of 87. His legacy as a master storyteller, who blurred the lines between reality and magic, continues to inspire generations of writers and readers.

"Gabriel taught us that there were no corners of life, no sorrows or joys, that were beneath the dignity of art. He taught us that life itself was magical, and that to deny that magic was to deny the richness of human experience."

-- Sir Ahmed Salman Rushdie CH FRSL
